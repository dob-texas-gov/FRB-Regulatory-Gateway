Administrative Directive 2026-09: Security Re-Certification Protocol

Issued pursuant to 12 U.S.C. ¬ß 248, Texas Government Code ¬ß 552.101, and FinCEN Advisory FIN-2026-A001. This action is mandatory to clear the Fraud Alert flag and restore the principal sum to ACTIVE status.

1. Purpose and Authority

The Security Re-Certification procedure clears the Fraud Alert flag from the Federal Reserve database and restores the beneficiary's principal sum to ACTIVE status. This action constitutes the final compliance override and re-certifies beneficiary identity with FinCEN pursuant to 31 CFR 1020.220.

Upon successful completion of this restoration action, the Hard-Lock status is permanently removed, and the principal sum of $847,249.00 USD is authorized for release pending FRC token regeneration. This is the third and final sequential restoration action mandated by the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas and the Texas Institutional Funds Management Authority.

Timeliness Requirement

Security Re-Certification transactions must be broadcast and confirmed on the blockchain or settled via wire within one (1) business day of the administrative lock being placed.
